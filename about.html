Soham Bakshi

Statistical Analyst and Researcher

I am a PhD student in Statistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, advised by Prof. Snigdha Panigrahi. My research interests lie in the interface of theoretical machine learning and statistical inference.

Research Interests

I am interested in creating and implementing innovative statistical techniques for inference after adaptive modeling of data. Currently, my research focuses on developing and applying selective inference methods for time-varying effect modification problems.

My expertise includes:

  • Machine Learning Theory
  • Statistical Inference
  • Uncertainty Quantification
  • Mathematical Modeling

Academic Background

I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics (Honours) from the Indian Statistical Institute, Bangalore Centre in 2022, graduating with First Division with Distinction. Currently, I am pursuing my PhD in Statistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

As a Graduate Student Instructor at the University of Michigan, I teach probability and machine learning, focusing on real-world applications in data analysis and decision-making.

Personal

Beyond my academic pursuits, I enjoy mentoring students in mathematics and statistics. I've trained high school students and undergraduates for mathematics olympiads and international competitions.

I am fluent in English and Bengali (native), with intermediate proficiency in Hindi.
